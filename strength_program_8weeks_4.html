8-Week Strength Program

40 min sessions Push / Pull / Legs Linear + Wave Progression 1RM Testing Protocol
Program Structure

How This Works

Three sessions per week following a Push / Pull / Legs split. Each session runs 38–42 minutes and is built around one primary compound lift, two secondary lifts, and one or two targeted accessories. Strength is built through a combination of accumulation (adding volume) and intensification (adding load), structured in two 4-week phases.

1
Weeks 1–4 · Accumulation

Build the Base

Higher rep ranges (5–8), moderate loads, strict technique. The goal is to groove movement patterns, accumulate quality volume, and establish your working weights. Week 4 is a deload — 60% of working loads, 2×5 per compound.

2
Weeks 5–8 · Intensification

Drive the Load

Heavier loads (3–5 reps), lower volume, more CNS demand. Strength peaks here. Week 8 finishes with the testing protocol — compare directly to Week 1 baselines to measure your gains.

Key Principles

Rules of the Program

RPE-Based Loading

Prescribed loads use RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion). This keeps loading honest on good and bad days.

RPE 7 — 3 reps left in tank RPE 8 — 2 reps left RPE 9 — 1 rep left RPE 10 — max effort

Progressive Overload Rule

If you complete all reps across all sets with RPE ≤ prescribed, add weight next session: +2.5 kg for upper body, +5 kg for lower body. If you miss reps, repeat the same weight.

Session Structure (40 min)

  • Warm-up (5 min) — 2 ramp-up sets on the primary lift (50%, 70% of working weight × 5 reps each)
  • Primary compound (12–15 min) — main strength work
  • Secondary lifts (18–20 min) — 2 exercises, less rest needed
  • Accessories (5 min) — 1–2 exercises, high density

Load Management

Deload & Recovery

Week 4 — Planned Deload

Reduce all compound lifts to 60% of working weight. Perform 2 sets of 5 reps only. Keep accessories light. This is mandatory — do not skip. The deload drives adaptation from Phase 1 and primes you for the heavier Phase 2 loads.

Auto-Regulation Rule

If you're feeling beaten up at any point (poor sleep, illness, high stress), drop all working loads by 10% that session. Don't push through on a bad day — it delays recovery more than skipping a small load increase.

Measuring Progress

Strength Assessment Protocol

Use this protocol at the start of Week 1 (baseline) and again at the end of Week 8 (retest). Compare directly. You are testing Squat, Chest Press Machine, and Deadlift — the three primary lifts of this program.

What You're Measuring

An estimated 1-rep max (e1RM), calculated from a heavy set using the Brzycki formula: e1RM = weight × (36 / (37 − reps)). You perform a 3–5RM effort — never a true single — which is safer and equally informative.

1

Day Before: Rest

Do not train the day before testing. 8+ hours sleep. Normal nutrition — don't fast or carb-load, just eat normally.

2

10-Minute Warm-Up

5 min light cardio. Then for each lift: bar × 10, 40% × 5, 60% × 3, 75% × 2, 85% × 1 (all estimated). Rest 2 min between ramp sets.

3

Working Attempt: 3–5RM

Select a weight you believe you can lift 3–5 times with excellent form. Perform the set, stopping one rep before failure. Record the weight and reps. Rest 5 minutes. If you got 6+ reps, add weight and repeat.

4

Calculate e1RM

Apply: e1RM = weight × (36 ÷ (37 − reps))

Example: 100 kg × 4 reps → 100 × (36 / 33) = 109 kg

5

Test All Three Lifts

Run the protocol for Squat, Chest Press Machine, and Deadlift — either in one session (with 10 min rest between lifts) or across two days (Squat/Chest Press one day, Deadlift the next).

6

Log and Compare

Record results in the My Lifts tab. Compare Week 8 e1RM to Week 1 baseline. A well-executed 8-week program should produce 5–15% gains on primary lifts for an intermediate lifter.

Secondary Indicators

Beyond e1RM, track these week to week as supporting evidence of progress:

  • Working weight increases — how many times did you add load to the bar across 8 weeks?
  • Rep quality — are your reps faster and more controlled at the same weight?
  • Session RPE — does a weight that was RPE 8 in Week 1 feel like RPE 7 in Week 7?
  • Pull-up reps — track max unweighted reps at Week 1 and Week 8.
